The above plots show the decay of U238 to Pb206, which seems to follow a simple nuclear decay dynamic.

Because the intermediate species have a halflife very small compared to the parent nuclei, in that time scale

its almost as if U238 directly gets converted to Pb206

hence the increase in the ratio when most of the U238 has decayed



Due to the super-long life time of U238, it is impractical to compare its fraction to other intermediate

isotopes because it would remain almost constant and we wouldnt be able make any determination from it.

Rather its more interesting to consider U234 and Th230 due to their long halflife (but not too long)

to probe for the age of rocks.



Ratio of the above two species vs time



The above plot shows the decrease in the ratio as the U234 slowly decays to Th230 while Th230 is already in a steady state due to its shorter half-life

The ratio of these isotopes found in rocks can be compared to give estimate of the age of the rock.

Time on the x-axis matching the ration will thus be the age of the rock
